About

About our hackathon!

The Academies of Loudoun Hackathon is held by the Computer Science Honor Society. At this event, participants learn at workshops and apply their knowledge during competitions. No prior CS experience is needed. The competition is completely free to participants, food is provided, and over $2,000 in prizes are rewarded. This is our first year holding a middle/high school hackathon. The 12 hour hackathon will consist of an opening ceremony, workshops, programming time, and an awards ceremony.

Structure

There will be 3 divisions: middle school, high school (have not taken AP Computer Science), and advanced high school (have taken AP Computer Science). Prizes will be awarded in every division. If you currently do not have a team, we will pair you with other students in your division. More information will be given during opening ceremonies.

During the programming portion, teams of up to four will receive a a challenge. The challenges are designed so that, even with simple knowledge of programming, you will be able to complete them.
